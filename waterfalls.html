Waterfalls 

Dunhinda Waterfall (Badulla)

• Key things to experience:

  • A scenic 1 km forest walk to the waterfall
  • Watching the misty spray that gives it the name “Dunhinda” (meaning smoky spray)
  • Great for nature photography and birdwatching

• Best time to visit: June to August (Water flow is strong after monsoon rains)


Ravana Ella Waterfall (Ella)

• Key things to experience:

  • Admire the cascading falls from the Ella-Wellawaya road
  • Combine with a visit to nearby Ravana Cave, linked to the Ramayana legend
  • Capture sunrise photos from the Ella side

• Best time to visit: March to May or September to November (before and after peak monsoon for safe access)


Olu Ella Falls (Yatiyantota, Kegalle District)

• Key things to experience:

  • Peaceful, less touristy atmosphere
  • Nature walk through small forest paths
  • Spotting endemic flora and fauna

• Best time to visit: May to October


Bopath Ella Falls (Kuruwita, Ratnapura District)

• Key things to experience:

  • Iconic shape of a Bo leaf (Bodhi leaf), giving it the name "Bopath"
  • Popular picnic site for locals
  • Refreshing dip in lower pools during dry months

• Best time to visit: August to October


Thudugala Ella Waterfall (Kalutara District)

• Key things to experience:

  • Small natural pool ideal for bathing
  • Explore the nearby old British-era estate ruins
  • A great family-friendly waterfall experience

• Best time to visit: June to October


Tips and Advice

• Before you Travel:

  • Trails can be slippery, especially after rain — wear non-slip hiking shoes or sandals with good grip.
  • A dry bag is useful to protect electronics from spray.
  • Only swim in designated safe areas — some pools have strong currents or hidden rocks.
  • Avoid visiting during heavy rains — trails may flood or be unsafe.
  • Avoid using soap or shampoo in natural pools.
  • Waterfalls like Thudugala Ella or Bopath Ella are family-friendly.

Waterfall Routes for Travelers

  • 1. Dunhinda Waterfall – Badulla From Colombo:
    Colombo → Kandy → Nuwara Eliya → Badulla (approx. 6–7 hrs by car)
    Or take the scenic train from Colombo to Badulla.
    Last Leg: Short hike (~1.5 km) from Dunhinda Falls entrance to the waterfall.

  •   2. Ravana Ella Waterfall – Ella From Colombo:
    Colombo → Ratnapura → Wellawaya → Ella (approx. 5–6 hrs by car)
    Or take the Colombo–Ella train (via Badulla route). From Kandy:
    Kandy → Nuwara Eliya → Ella (~5 hrs)
    Tip: The falls are beside the main road – no hike needed. 

  •  3. Olu Ella Falls – Yatiyanthota, Kegalle District From Colombo:
    Colombo → Avissawella → Yatiyanthota → Olu Ella (~3.5 hrs)
    From Kandy: Kandy → Gampola → Nawalapitiya → Yatiyanthota (~3.5 hrs)
    Note: Road is narrow near the waterfall; plan for daytime travel. 

  •  4. Bopath Ella Falls – Kuruwita, Ratnapura District From Colombo:
    Colombo → Avissawella → Kuruwita (~2.5 hrs by car)
    From Ratnapura: Just 20–30 min from Ratnapura town Short walk (~500m) from the entrance to the falls. 

  •  5. Thudugala Ella Waterfall – Kalutara District From Colombo:
    Colombo → Kalutara → Dodangoda → Thudugala (~2.5 hrs)
    From Galle: Galle → Elpitiya → Matugama → Thudugala (~2.5 hrs) Access: Easy walk through a forest trail (~10 mins)
