Our Travel Journal

Discover the best of Sri Lanka through our curated stories and articles.


   
   

Exploring the Ancient Wonders of Sigiriya

Climb the magnificent Lion Rock and discover the ancient frescoes and gardens of one of Sri Lanka's most iconic historical sites. Our expert guides will take you through a journey of ancient royal history...



Wildlife Safari in Yala National Park.

Witness leopards, elephants, and a wide array of birds in their natural habitat on our guided safari...

Conquering the Sacred Adam's Peak at Sunrise

Embark on a spiritual pilgrimage and be rewarded with a breathtaking sunrise view from the summit of Adam's Peak, a site revered by many religions for centuries.

The Ancient City of Polonnaruwa                                           

Polonnaruwa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was Sri Lanka's second ancient capital. This remarkable city is home to a vast collection of well-preserved ruins, including the Royal Palace, temples, and large stone statues.

The Iconic Nine Arch Bridge in Ella

A marvel of colonial-era engineering, the iconic Nine Arch Bridge is a must-see attraction in the scenic hills of Ella. Built entirely of stone and brick, this beautiful bridge is surrounded by lush tea plantations, making it a favorite spot for photographers and travelers.

The Historic Galle Fort    

Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a spectacular blend of history and modern life. Originally built by the Portuguese and fortified by the Dutch, its ancient ramparts, cobbled streets, and colonial-era architecture are now home to unique cafes, boutiques, and art galleries.

Tea Plantations of Nuwara Eliya                                 

Nuwara Eliya, known as "Little England," is famous for its cool climate and endless tea plantations. Visitors can enjoy the breathtaking views of green hillsides and learn about the tea-making process at a local factory.

 

Whale Watching in Mirissa

Witness magnificent blue whales and playful dolphins on an unforgettable boat safari off the southern coast of Mirissa. A truly magical experience for any nature lover.

The Golden Temple of Dambulla

Explore the stunning UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Dambulla Cave Temple, a massive rock sanctuary filled with ancient Buddhist murals and over 150 statues of the Buddha..

Trekking at Horton Plains

Hike through the scenic landscapes of Horton Plains National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and reach the stunning viewpoint of World's End for a breathtaking panorama.

Discover the Spices of Sri Lanka

Take a tour through the vibrant spice gardens of Matale, where you can learn about the cultivation of cinnamon, vanilla, and various medicinal herbs and spices
