Diverse Destinations

Your ‘Must-See’ Places

With myriad places to visit, Sri Lanka is a little paradise isle full of surprising variety. You can swap the chaos of a city for a tranquil beach in an hour or less, or switch from tropical heat to cool heights and cloud forests for a few more. There are mountains to climb, waves to surf, whales to watch, history to revisit, jungles to explore, even elephants to meet. The destinations in Sri Lanka are wide and varied and we’ll gladly share them with you in the very best way.



Siripade

Sri Pada (Adam’s Peak) is a sacred mountain in Sri Lanka, known for a footprint-shaped mark at its summit. Revered by multiple religions, it draws thousands of pilgrims each year, especially during the December–May season.

National Shrine of Our Lady of Madhu

Sri Lanka’s most sacred Catholic shrine, nestled in a forest in Mannar. A peaceful pilgrimage site with deep spiritual significance, attracting devotees from all over the island.

Nilaveli Beach

A stunning stretch of white sand and crystal-clear waters near Trincomalee. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and taking boat trips to nearby Pigeon Island for snorkeling.

 Velgam Vehera Buddhist Temple

An ancient Buddhist temple near Trincomalee, dating back to the Anuradhapura era. Surrounded by forest, it offers a peaceful setting and unique stone ruins.

Thirukoneswaram Kovil

A historic Hindu temple perched on Swami Rock in Trincomalee. Known for its dramatic ocean views and deep spiritual significance to Shaivite Hindus.

Crocodile Lake – Panama

A quiet, scenic wetland near Arugam Bay, home to wild crocodiles and exotic birds. Best visited with a local guide, it offers a raw, untamed look at Sri Lanka’s wildlife.

Ridi Viharaya

An ancient cave temple near Kurunegala, built during the reign of King Dutugemunu. Known for its rock inscriptions, paintings, and serene rural setting.

Yapahuwa Rock Fortress

A 13th-century rock fortress and former capital with a majestic stone staircase. Offers panoramic views and a glimpse into medieval Sri Lankan architecture.

Kalpitiya Dutch Church

A colonial-era church built by the Dutch in the 17th century. Though partly in ruins, it stands as a quiet testament to the region’s colonial past.

Sri Munneswaram Devalaya

An important multi-religious temple complex near Chilaw. Revered by both Hindus and Buddhists, it hosts vibrant festivals and centuries of history.

Wilpattu National Park

Sri Lanka’s largest national park, famous for its unique “willus” (natural lakes) and diverse wildlife including leopards, elephants, and sloth bears. Ideal for safari adventures.

Dambulla Royal Cave Temple
