Diverse Destinations

Your ‘Must-See’ Places

With myriad places to visit, Sri Lanka is a little paradise isle full of surprising variety. You can swap the chaos of a city for a tranquil beach in an hour or less, or switch from tropical heat to cool heights and cloud forests for a few more. There are mountains to climb, waves to surf, whales to watch, history to revisit, jungles to explore, even elephants to meet. The destinations in Sri Lanka are wide and varied and we’ll gladly share them with you in the very best way.



Belilena Cave

Located in Kitulgala, Belilena is a historic limestone cave where ancient human remains over 30,000 years old were discovered. Surrounded by forest, it’s a great spot for light trekking and exploring Sri Lanka’s early human history.

Nelligala International Buddhist Centre

Perched atop a hill in Kandy, Nelligala offers stunning panoramic views and a peaceful atmosphere. Known for its golden statues and modern temple architecture, it's a serene place for worship and reflection.

Post Office - Nuwara Eliya

The Nuwara Eliya Post Office is a historic landmark located in the heart of Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka. Established in 1894, it is one of the oldest post offices in the country and a prime example of Tudor Revival architecture, featuring red brick walls, orange roofs, and a distinctive clock tower

Hikkaduwa Beach

Famous for its pristine sandy shores and vibrant coral reefs, Hikkaduwa is a top destination for snorkeling, diving, and surfing. The beach buzzes with lively cafes, shops, and nightlife, making it perfect for both adventure and relaxation.

Kalametiya Lagoon Bird Sanctuary & Wetland Park

Established in 1938, Kalametiya is Sri Lanka’s oldest coastal wetland sanctuary, home to over 150 bird species, including rare migratory and endemic birds. Visitors can explore its tranquil mangroves, reed beds, and salt marshes by boat, enjoying excellent birdwatching and peaceful nature walks.

Jungle Beach

A hidden gem nestled within lush greenery, Jungle Beach offers a quiet, secluded stretch of golden sand. Known for its clear waters and vibrant marine life, it’s perfect for snorkeling, swimming, and relaxing away from the crowds.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sinharaja is Sri Lanka’s last primary rainforest. Renowned for its rich biodiversity, it shelters many endemic plants and animals. Visitors can trek through dense jungle trails to discover waterfalls, rare birds, and unique wildlife.

Thalpe Beach

A tranquil, less crowded beach known for its soft golden sands and calm waters. Ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and peaceful strolls, it offers a relaxed atmosphere away from busy tourist spots.

Sea turtle hatchery habaraduwa

A conservation center protecting endangered sea turtles. Visitors can see hatchlings, learn about turtle species, and join in releasing baby turtles into the sea. A great spot for families and nature lovers.

Nagadeepa Temple

A sacred Buddhist temple located on Nagadeepa Island, known for its ancient history and spiritual significance. The temple attracts pilgrims who come to worship and enjoy the peaceful island setting.

Elephantpass

A narrow strip of land connecting Sri Lanka’s mainland to the Jaffna Peninsula. Historically significant, it was named by the Dutch during colonial times when elephants were transported across the lagoon. Today, it serves as a vital transportation route and a poignant reminder of the country’s complex history.

Nallur Kandaswamy Devasthanam

A prominent Hindu temple in Jaffna dedicated to Lord Murugan. Known for its vibrant festivals and stunning Dravidian architecture, it serves as a major religious and cultural center in northern Sri Lanka.
