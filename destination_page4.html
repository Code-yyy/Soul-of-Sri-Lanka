Diverse Destinations

Your ‘Must-See’ Places

With myriad places to visit, Sri Lanka is a little paradise isle full of surprising variety. You can swap the chaos of a city for a tranquil beach in an hour or less, or switch from tropical heat to cool heights and cloud forests for a few more. There are mountains to climb, waves to surf, whales to watch, history to revisit, jungles to explore, even elephants to meet. The destinations in Sri Lanka are wide and varied and we’ll gladly share them with you in the very best way.



Mihintale Raja Maha Viharaya

The cradle of Buddhism in Sri Lanka, where Emperor Ashoka’s mission first arrived. Features ancient monasteries, stupas, and panoramic hilltop views.

Minneriya National Park

Renowned for its massive elephant gatherings during the dry season. Visitors can enjoy thrilling safaris to see elephants, birds, and other wildlife.

Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi

One of the oldest sacred fig trees in the world, located in Anuradhapura. A deeply revered Buddhist pilgrimage site symbolizing enlightenment.

Bopath Falls

A picturesque waterfall in the shape of a sacred Bo leaf, located in Kuruwita. Easily accessible and surrounded by lush greenery, it’s a popular picnic and bathing spot.

Makandawa Reserved Forest

A dense rainforest near Kitulgala, rich in biodiversity and home to rare birds and butterflies. Great for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring unspoiled nature.

Pinnawala Zoo

A small, modern zoo near the famous Elephant Orphanage. Features native wildlife in open enclosures, offering a family-friendly educational experience.

Olu Ella Falls

One of the tallest waterfalls in Sri Lanka, hidden within lush forest near Yatiyanthota. Less crowded and ideal for nature lovers seeking a serene escape.

Nine Arches Bridge

An iconic colonial-era railway bridge in Ella, surrounded by lush hills and tea plantations. A popular spot for photos, especially when trains pass over the arches.

Yala National Park

Sri Lanka’s most visited national park, known for its leopard population, elephants, and coastal lagoons. Offers unforgettable jeep safaris through diverse landscapes.

Dunhinda Waterfall

A majestic waterfall near Badulla, cascading through misty forest. Reached by a short scenic hike, it’s one of the most beautiful falls in the country.

Ravana Ella Waterfall

A dramatic roadside waterfall linked to the legend of King Ravana. Located near Ella, it's easily accessible and a favorite stop for travelers in the hill country.

Lipton’s Seat

A scenic viewpoint in Haputale where Sir Thomas Lipton once surveyed his tea empire. Offers breathtaking panoramic views of tea plantations, valleys, and distant mountains.
